John Paparrizos is an assistant professor at The Ohio State University, leading The DATUM Lab. His research focuses on adaptive solutions for data-intensive and machine-learning applications. His doctoral work was recognized at the 2019 ACM SIGKDD Doctoral Dissertation Award competition. He has received multiple other distinctions, including the inaugural ACM SIGMOD Research Highlight Award, a NetApp Faculty Award, and the 2023 IEEE TCDE Rising Star Award. John's work has been featured in popular media outlets, including The New York Times, Washington Post, Guardian, and MIT Technology Review. His ideas have been widely adopted across scientific areas, Fortune 100-500 companies (e.g., Exelon and Nokia), and organizations such as the European Space Agency. John completed his Ph.D. at Columbia University and received postdoctoral training at the University of Chicago.
